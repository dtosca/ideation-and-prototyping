--- layout: post title: "The Rubik's Cube: simple and complex" author: Diana Tosca permalink: /50-shades/ date: 2021-10-04 ---

Growing up, I always had a Rubik's Cube lying around the house. I found it to be a marvelous puzzle, simple and complex. Even my dad (the smartest man in the world) couldn't figure out it's mysteries! It wasn't until I met my wife, a Rubik’s cube enthusiast, that I started to uncover the secrets of the cube. Learning the algorithms to solve the cube is a deceptively simple process, but the math behind the solutions are complex.

In making the fifty renderings of the cube, I realized the drawings involved the same deceptively simple process as solving it.

It’s a straightforward figure: one cube, six faces, nine squares in each face, and twelve edges. Except I learned how hard it was to perfectly capture its simplicity.

Drawing a straight line is difficult, the angles and edges are hard to get just right. My hand and my eyes don’t seem to want to cooperate!

I had to let go of the perfection that I was striving for when making sketches. Instead, I should appreciate the process.
